## D. Roadmap / Future Enhancements

### Google Review Response Workflow

**Goal:** Respond to Google reviews thoughtfully with owner approval — not auto-posted.

**Recommended approach:**
1. **Monitoring:** Set up Google Business Profile email notifications for new reviews. Owner gets an email whenever a review is posted.
2. **Draft responses:** Use a simple system (Google Sheet, email thread, or dedicated tool) where response drafts are written and sent to the owner for approval.
3. **Positive reviews:** Draft a warm thank-you. Include specifics from the review (e.g., "Glad the oak removal went smoothly — that was a tricky one near the deck"). Add a slight delay (12-24 hours) so it doesn't feel robotic.
4. **Negative reviews:** Flag immediately. Draft a professional, non-defensive response. Encourage offline resolution ("We'd love to make this right — please call us at..."). Owner must approve before posting.
5. **Tools:** For a small business, this doesn't need software. A shared Google Doc or email chain where drafts are reviewed is sufficient. If volume grows, tools like Podium or BrightLocal offer review management with approval workflows, but they're overkill at this stage.

**Difficulty:** Easy to implement manually. The workflow is: notification → draft → owner reviews → post. No code required.

**What's needed from client:** Google Business Profile access (owner must be the profile owner). Agreement on response tone and turnaround time.
